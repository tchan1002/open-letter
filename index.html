Two years ago, I had the opportunity to work in Hong Kong for a summer, where my grandparents still live. That summer, in fact, they were my roommates. We lived in one room, outfitted with a balcony which served as clothes-line, showerstall, and bathroom. Used to the amenities of space, it felt odd to compress myself into so little. The heat stifled.

I would only realize later that this was a destructive instinct. A surprisingly human one at that. It's a philosophy of ownership that turns into expansion. The internet is a new frontier, and like any space, we must consider our relationship with it. To try and own it, to section off its parts is to enter a chain reaction of destruction...
